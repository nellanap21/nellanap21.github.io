How to fill out the purchase contract

This guide shows you how to fill out a real estate contract. I’ll actually walk line by line through the contract we signed for a house we purchased last year. We used a real estate agent last year, so this was filled out by our agent.

Fill out the contract

Here is my completed contract so you can see exactly what we had to fill out.

Close of escrow is the date you officially become the owner of the property. It’s also called the “closing”. Typically this is 30 days.

Expiration of Offer means that the seller has to accept within 3 days.

Initial Deposit is the amount of money you will transfer within 3 days to the Escrow Agent (it will count towards your down payment). It’s also called the “Earnest Money” or “Good Faith Deposit”

Loan Amount is the amount you plan to finance with a mortgage.

Occupancy Type is Primary if you plan to live in the house.

Down Payment is the amount of cash you wire on the day of closing.

At the bottom, you have the Purchase Price Total.

Loan Contingency is the time you need to get your loan approved by the bank. 17 days is standard.

Appraisal Contingency is the time you need to get the appraisal report from the bank. 17 days is standard.

Investigation of Property is the time you need to complete your inspections. 17 days is standard.

Review of Seller Documents is the review of seller’s disclosures, reports, and other information.

Preliminary Title Report is the time you need to obtain the preliminary report, which is an offer by the title insurer to issue a title insurance policy.

Items Included in the sale of the property are checked. If the house has any of these appliances, they would be included in the sale.

Natural Hazard Zone Disclosure is paid by seller.

Smoke alarms, CO detectors and water heater bracing must be present before the sale closes. Seller typically pays for those before the sale closes.

Escrow Fee is charged by the escrow agent to facilitate the transaction.

Owner’s title insurance policy is the insurance that covers your ownership of the property.

County transfer tax is the tax you pay to purchase the property.

HOA transfer fees is the homeowner’s association’s fees to transfer the property.

Home warranty plan covers replacements and repairs of major appliances.

Advisories provide guidance on common issues.

Buyer’s Investigation Advisory tells you the importance of doing a careful investigation of the property.

California Consumer Privacy Act Advisory tells you that your personal information will be shared with transaction parties during the sale.

Fair Housing and Discrimination Advisory tells you that discrimination is prohibited in a property transaction

Loan contingency means in ability to obtain a loan allows you to cancel the contract

Appraisal contingency gives you the right to cancel if the appraised value is less than the purchase price.

Investigation contingency gives you you right to cancel if condition of property is not as you expected

Existing fixtures and fittings are typically included in the sale.

Legally required installations of smoke alarms, carbon monoxide detectors, and water heater strapping must be done before closing.

Home warranty plan is paid by seller up to a negotiated amount. Any additional amounts are paid by buyer.

Megan’s Law Database allows you to check if sex offenders reside nearby.

Solar Power Systems require additional disclosure from the seller

Inspections include home inspections, pest inspections, and any other inspections

Inability to obtain insurance counts as investigation contingency

Repair request or concession can be made if you discovery an issue during inspections

Contingency removal letter shall be delivered to the seller if there are no issues

Seller can cancel if buyer doesn’t remove the contingencies.

Seller can cancel if buyer breaches the contract (e.g. not depositing funds)

Buyer can cancel if seller breaches the contract (e.g. not delivering disclosures

Buyer can cancel by exercising any contingency

Removing a contingency means you have completed your investigations, removed your cancellation rights, and elected to proceed.

Final verification of condition allows you to confirm that the property has been maintained in substantially the same state as when contract was signed

You need to deliver the agreement to the escrow holder within 3 days. Any amendment needs to be delivered within 3 days.

As Is means that seller needs to disclose known material facts and defects

Liquidated Damages means that if you default and fail to complete the purchase, seller can keep the initial deposit.

Arbitration of Disputes means that you agree to have disputes decided by a neutral arbitrator.

Expiration of Offer means your offer is revoked unless buyer accepts within the deadline.

Buyer Signatures - this is where you sign and date.

Real Estate Broker Section is only applicable if you have a real estate broker.
