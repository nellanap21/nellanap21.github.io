How to get preapproval

The first step in buying a home is to get a pre-approval letter from the bank.

During the pre-approval process, you’ll fill out an application and provide documents to verify financial information in the application. Then the underwriter will look at your income, assets, and credit score to determine how much you can borrow.

There is a big difference between a pre-approval and a pre-qualification.

A pre-qualification can be obtained by filling out a quick form and simply estimates how much you may be able to borrow. A pre-approval letter is only obtained after an initial underwriting, so a pre-approval will make your bid stronger than a pre-qualification letter.

Here are the steps to a pre-approval letter.

  1. Fill out the application
  2. Gather the supplemental docs
  3. Provide follow-up docs
  4. Get an offer for how much you can borrow
  5. Ask for the pre-approval letter
  6. Review your pre-approval letter

Let's take it from the top

1. Fill out the application

In order for the bank to begin working on your preapproval letter, you need to provide a completed application, as well as some supporting documents.

Here is my completed application so you can see exactly what I had to provide.

Section 1a is all about your personal information.

Check Joint Credit if you and your spouse are both going to be borrowers. You’ll both need to provide info for both borrowers.

Section 1b is about your current employment

Section 1c also includes self employment, previous employment, and other sources of income.

List as many sources of incomes as possible to strengthen your application. Here we listed rental income from our two other properties.

Section 2 is about your net worth. You have to list out your assets and liabilities.

For assets, include every bank, retirement, and other account.

For liabilities, include any debt you currently owe (car, student loan, credit cards).

Section 3 is about any real estate that you own. For any rental properties list your monthly rental income.

For the property value, I listed the current price on Zillow or Redfin (whichever one was higher).

Section 4 is about the property you want to purchase.

You have to list whether you will be living in the property as a primary residence or renting it out.

Section 5 is about how you will use the property and where you will be getting the funding.

If you are borrowing money or getting a gift for the down payment, you’ll have to note that here.

The acknowledgements in Section 6 are where you promise that all of the information you provided is true, accurate, and complete

Section 7 is where you can list your military service.

Section 8 is where you can provide your demographic information. You can decline to answer this if you don’t want to fill it out.

Section 9 is information about the loan originator. You don’t need to fill this out.

When you have a joint borrower, you fill out a separate form for the additional borrower.

Include your personal and employment information.

You won’t need to repeat your assets, liabilities, and real estate because it is the same as the primary borrower.

For Section 5, you do have to make these declarations again for the additional borrower.

Finally, Section 8 is optional and Section 9 is completed by the bank.

2. Gather the supplemental docs

Here is the list of documents we needed as support for the application.

Supplementary Documents/Items:

  • W2 from the past two years for all applicants
  • Recent paystub and year-end paystubs for 2021 band 2022 for all applicants
  • Statements for assets and liabilities held outside of Citi (bank account, brokerage, retirement, loans, etc.)
  • Schedule E for any investment real estate holdings

3. Provide follow-up docs

After you have submitted the loan application, you might receive additional questions or document requests.

4. Get an offer for how much you can borrow

Then the bank will actually give you an amount that they are willing to lend you.

In this case, we wanted to borrow more but the bank was only willing to lend $500k.

Once you have found a home you want to bid on, ask for a pre-approval letter in the exact amount of the amount you plan to borrow.
