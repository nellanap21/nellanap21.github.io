A prequalification is an initial assessment of your financial situation. This helps determine the approximate loan amount you might qualify for, based on income, assets, debts, and credit history.

‍

The first step in buying a home is to get a prequalification letter from the bank.

During the pre-approval process, youâ€™ll fill out an application and provide documents to verify financial information in the application. Then the underwriter will look at your income, assets, and credit score to determine how much you can borrow.

A prequalification is a quicker and more informal version of a preapproval.

You can get prequalified by filling out a form and getting an auto-generated estimate of how much you can afford.

A pre-approval letter is only obtained after an initial underwriting, so a pre-approval will make your bid stronger than a prequalification letter.

Here are the steps to a pre-approval letter.

  1. Fill out loan application
  2. Gather supporting docs
  3. Provide follow-up docs
  4. Get your loan amount
  5. Ask for your letter
  6. Review your letter

1. Fill out loan application

First, reach out to the bank, or other lender, to get a copy of the loan application.

Here are some tips for putting your best foot forward when filling out the application.

List as many sources of incomes as possible to strengthen your application. This could be side hustles, rental properties, or anything else.

For assets, include every bank, retirement, and other account. It's worth the trouble of finding old logins to list every single asset.

Don't forget to include your joint borrower too.

2. Gather supporting docs

In addition to answering questions in the application, you also have to provide supporting documents to prove your income (the W2) and assets (the bank statements).

These are the supporting document I had to provide:

  • W2 from the past two years for all applicants
  • Recent paystub and year-end paystubs for 2021 band 2022 for all applicants

5. Ask for your letter

Once you have found a home you want to bid on, ask for a prequalification letter in the exact amount you plan to borrow.

If you get a prequalication letter for more than the exact amount you need, then the seller knows that you can potentially increasde your bid by just borrowing more.
