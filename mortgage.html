How to handle mortgage contingency

The mortgage contingency requires you to go through formal underwriting by your bank and receive an official loan commitment.

Since you’ve already started a loan application with your bank (see the page on getting the pre-approval letter), you don’t need to provide that information again.

Here, I’ll show you how I received a loan commitment and then removed the loan contingency.

  1. Check the program overview
  2. Review the Borrower’s Checklist
  3. Get home insurance
  4. Review additional info requested by underwriter
  5. Send additional info
  6. Get full approval for the loan

Let's take it from the top

1. Check the program overview

Rate Commitment means you can’t lock your interest rate until you have a fully signed contract.

Selection of Property means you have to provide the signed contract and any amendments to the contract

Final Loan Commitment is what you are looking to get. It’s promise to give you the mortgage at closing.

2. Review the Borrower’s Checklist

As soon as you sign, send the contract to the bank so they can start the process.

Provide bank statements to prove you have enough money for down payment, closing costs, and reserves

Send the bank any amendments to the contract

3. Get home insurance

The bank requires you to get home insurance. Get a quote and then introduce the insurance agent to the loan officer.

4. Review additional info requested by underwriter

Now that you have the signed contract, the underwriter is going to request more explanations or documents.

We signed on Oct 11 and got the first requests on Oct 16.

5. Send requested additional info

This is an example of how you can respond to a request for additional explanation.

6. Get full approval for the loan

This is what you’ve been waiting for! Now you can remove the mortgage contingency.
