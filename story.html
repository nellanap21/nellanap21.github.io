Hi, my name is Allen, and I started Home 101 to increase home ownership in the United States. I see my mission as teaching Americans everything I know about buying a home. Purchasing a home is a key path to building wealth in America, but this American Dream has become difficult with high interest rates and prices. The recent NAR settlement has created a new opportunity for Americans to buy a home without having to pay 3% commission. That's enough for a 3.5% down payment with an FHA loan!

I've been lucky enough to buy 4 homes in my life. This was because I had to move from New York, to New Jersey, and most recently to California. Every time I purchased a home, I learned a ton, and that is what I'd like to share with you.

All homeowners have to start somewhere, right? Let‚Äôs see how I got started.

Renting in NYC Sucked

Ever since I graduated from school and started my first job in 2012, I‚Äôve always wanted to own a home. To me, owning a home is a life achievement and part of the American dream.

At the time, I was living in a New York City apartment. Every year, the building manager would raise the rent by 10-15%, and the only way I could afford rent was to move to worse and worse apartments in the same building. Over 3 years, I moved from 16E, to 10E, and finally to 7B, which was literally the smallest unit on the lowest floor.

The NYC co-op

In 2015, my girlfriend and I wanted to stop paying rent so badly that we bought a small apartment. We weren‚Äôt even married at the time. Not having to pay for a wedding at that time helped with the down payment. üòä

I loved that 700 sq ft apartment. Sure, the windows were drafty and the view was of the brick wall next door, but it was a relief to have a place I could call my own.

The NJ house that changed my life

By 2021, I got married and had a baby. Our 1 bedroom apartment wasn‚Äôt going to be big enough, so we bought a house in the New Jersey suburbs.

For the first time in my life, I truly understood the power of owning your own home. It gave me peace of mind knowing I had a place to live and raise a family. And the freedom to do what I wanted on my land like creating my own bon fire.

It also gave me financial security. Mortgage payments felt way better than rent because the monthly payments on a 30 year fixed rate mortgage were never going to go up. This was huge safety net when inflation hit and although I had a monthly mortgage payment, I knew that part of that payment was paying down the loan (which is basically saving for retirement).

Moving to California

Less than 1 year later, I had to move to California and find a new house on short notice. I remember arriving in San Francisco on a Saturday and going to open houses on Sunday.

I quickly put a bid on a house and got an accepted offer. However, I learned a lot about my rights as a buyer when I discovered a previous landslide in the backyard and made the difficult decision to exercise a contingency to back out of the signed contract.

I ended up buying a different house off-market that needed a lot of work. We saved a lot of money by buying a fixer and doing a lot of the renovation ourselves.

The second home

Last year, I bought a second home because I wanted a place where my parents could live nearby and help out with the kid.

My experience buying the first 3 homes taught me to run a thorough inspection process. I found hidden problems and convinced the seller to reduce the purchase price by $40,000 so we could pay for the repairs.
